# A - Multi-Player Territory Game

## Overview
You (Player 0) play a turn-based, simultaneous-action territory game against $M-1$ AI players on an $N \times N$ grid. The goal is to maximize the ratio of your score to the highest score among the AI players.

## Grid & State
* **Grid**: $N \times N$ ($N=10$). Cells are 0-indexed $(i, j)$.
* **Players**: $M$ players in total ($2 \le M \le 8$). You are Player 0.
* **Turns**: $T = 100$ turns.
* **Cell Properties**: Each cell $(i, j)$ has:
  * **Value ($V_{i,j}$)**: A constant integer $\ge 1$. The sum of all $V_{i,j}$ is exactly $1000 \times N^2$. (Generated by randomly placing 0 to 2 peaks and normalizing).
  * **Owner ($O_{i,j}$)**: The player who owns it, or `-1` if unowned.
  * **Level ($L_{i,j}$)**: An integer from $0$ to $U$ ($1 \le U \le 5$). Unowned cells have level 0.
* **Pieces**: Each player controls exactly 1 piece, which is always located on one of their own territories.
* **Initial State**: Each player starts with a 1-cell territory at $(sx_p, sy_p)$ with level 1, and their piece is initially placed there.

## Turn Sequence
Each turn, players perform actions simultaneously through the following phases:
1. **Movement Decision**: 
   * Each player chooses a destination cell $(tx_p, ty_p)$ for their piece.
   * **Valid Destinations**: The cell must be reachable from the piece's current position by moving only through the player's own adjacent (up/down/left/right) territories, OR it must be adjacent to any cell in this reachable territory.
   * The destination must NOT currently contain another player's piece at the time of decision.
2. **Conflict Resolution**:
   * All pieces are moved to their chosen destinations.
   * If multiple pieces land on the same cell:
     * If the cell's owner has their piece there, that piece stays. All other pieces on the cell are "returned" (sent back to their starting positions for this turn).
     * If the cell is unowned, or the owner's piece is not there, ALL pieces on that cell are "returned".
3. **Territory Update**:
   * For each piece that was NOT returned:
     * **Occupy**: If the cell is unowned, it becomes the player's territory with level 1.
     * **Reinforce**: If the cell is already the player's territory, its level increases by 1 (up to the maximum level $U$).
     * **Attack**: If the cell belongs to another player, its level decreases by 1. If the level reaches 0, it becomes the attacking player's territory with level 1. If it does not reach 0, the attacking piece is "returned".
4. **Piece Return**:
   * Any piece that was returned during Conflict Resolution or Attack goes back to its position at the start of the turn.
   * *Note*: A cell with a piece currently on it cannot be attacked. A player's territory can never be reduced to 0 cells.

## AI Behavior
Each AI player $p$ ($1 \le p \le M-1$) has internal parameters $wa_p, wb_p, wc_p, wd_p, \varepsilon_p$.
For every valid destination $(i,j)$ in the AI's reachable set $B_p$, it calculates an evaluation score $A_{p,i,j}$:
* Unowned cell: $V_{i,j} \times wa_p$
* Own cell, level $< U$: $V_{i,j} \times wb_p$
* Own cell, level $= U$: $0$
* Other player's cell, level $= 1$: $V_{i,j} \times wc_p$
* Other player's cell, level $\ge 2$: $V_{i,j} \times wd_p$

The AI selects its destination by:
* **Random Action** (probability $\varepsilon_p$): Choosing uniformly at random from $B_p$.
* **Greedy Action** (probability $1 - \varepsilon_p$): Choosing the cell with the maximum $A_{p,i,j}$ (breaking ties uniformly at random).

## Scoring
After $T$ turns, player $p$'s score $S_p$ is the sum of $V_{i,j} \times L_{i,j}$ for all their owned cells (including unreachable ones).
Let $S_A = \max_{1 \le p \le M-1} S_p$.
Your absolute score for a testcase is:
$$\text{round}\left(10^5 \times \log_2\left(1 + \frac{S_0}{S_A}\right)\right)$$

## I/O Format

This problem is **non-interactive** on the official judge. The per-turn input below
is for the interactive explanation only. The local tester uses an offline format
and appends additional random numbers to the initial input.

### Interactive Format (Explanation)
Initial input:
```text
N M T U
V_{0,0} ... V_{0,N-1}
...
V_{N-1,0} ... V_{N-1,N-1}
sx_0 sy_0
...
sx_{M-1} sy_{M-1}
wa_1 wb_1 wc_1 wd_1 \varepsilon_1
...
wa_{M-1} wb_{M-1} wc_{M-1} wd_{M-1} \varepsilon_{M-1}
```

Per turn $t \in [1, T]$:
Output:
```text
tx_0 ty_0
```
Input:
```text
tx_1 ty_1
...
tx_{M-1} ty_{M-1}
ex_0 ey_0
...
ex_{M-1} ey_{M-1}
O_{0,0} ... O_{0,N-1}
...
O_{N-1,0} ... O_{N-1,N-1}
L_{0,0} ... L_{0,N-1}
...
L_{N-1,0} ... L_{N-1,N-1}
```
